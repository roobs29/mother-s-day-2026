I — IV

Four small chapters,
about everything you are.

Chapter One

You were there for every first.

You were there before the world even knew my name. The first heartbeat. The first breath. The first small cry you somehow translated into love. Every beginning I have is, in some quiet way, yours.

And you have been there, gently, at every threshold since.

Chapter Two

You carried more than anyone saw.

You carried weight no one was meant to see — late nights, quiet worries, prayers whispered into the dark. We grew up dressed in your sacrifices, and only now do we begin to understand what they cost.

Thank you for the things you never said you carried.

Chapter Three

Your love built this whole family.

You built our family the way artists build cathedrals — slowly, patiently, with hands that knew exactly where to place the light. Our home is your quiet masterpiece.

Every soft thing in our lives is something you taught us how to make.

Chapter Four

Home was always wherever you were.

It was never the walls or the address. Home was the smell of something on the stove, your voice from another room, the exact warmth of your shoulder against mine.

It still is.

A letter

The things I have been
trying to tell you.

Amma,

There are things I have been trying to tell you my whole life, and I think the reason I never could is because words always seemed too small for them.

I want you to know that I see you. Not the version the world sees — the practical, capable, ever-steady you — but the version who was tired and kept going. The version who let her own dreams quietly wait. The version who somehow always made room for ours.

I think about the small things now. The way you ask me for money without having any yourself. The way you'd hum without realising. The way you never talk about the slient battles I see you fight. The way you'd ask if I'd eaten before you asked anything else. I used to think those were ordinary. I know now they were the ordinary that everything else was built on.

I am, in every way I can be, the work of your love. The patience you taught me. The kindness you modelled before I had a name for it. The quiet, stubborn hope you somehow planted in me without me ever noticing.

If I ever build anything beautiful in my life, it will be with the tools you gave me.

Thank you for the meals I didn't thank you for. The arguments you let me win. The fears you carried so I wouldn't have to. The version of me you believed in long before I did.

You are the gentlest extraordinary thing in my life.

I love you, Amma. More than I have ever known how to say.

— always yours.
